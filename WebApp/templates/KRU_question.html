Bakery Hunt

It is Christmas, next week at Wonderland and Peter’s mom wants to buy two huge cakes for the party at their home. Since people like varieties, Peter’s mom decides to buy the cakes from two different bakeries in their town. Now there are N bakeries in the town with M bi-directional roads connecting them.

Now Peter and his brother Joe have to visit all the bakeries independently in order to find the best ones. Unfortunately, they don’t get along so well, and decide to take two different paths to all the bakeries. And since, no one likes to actually travel for a lot of time, they have decided to find the two least-time paths that cost. Note that two different paths mean that two paths have at least a road different.

Suppose they cannot find two such paths or even a single such path, they start fighting and their mother has to bake her own cakes and that costs a time of 10^9 + 7. Now print the sum of time of travel of the brother or 10^9 + 7 if they fight.

Constraints

1≤N,M≤103

1≤Weight Of Edge≤103

Input

First line consists of two integers N and M denoting the number of bakeries and the number of roads. The next Q lines consists of 3 integers, denoting the vertices of a un-directed edge and the corresponding edge weight.

Output

Print a single integer denoting the total time

Sample Input

 3 3
 0 1 1
 0 2 1
 2 1 1  
 3 6
 0 1 1
 0 3 1
 0 2 1
 2 1 1
 1 3 1
 2 3 1

Sample Output

 100000007
 6
